Hello, my name's Amanita! I am a queer latine venezuelan 21 year old artist who loves mycology, mythology(ha!), theology, horror, cryptozoology, the punk subculture and a bunch of other weird stuff! My native language is spanish so, expect some stuff to be in spanish.

They-Them pronouns, nonbinary in the way an anarchist fae mushroom cryptid or something should be!

The fungi’s mycelial (root) network also helps facilitate sharing of nutrients and information between various plants and trees of different species – this is cutely known as the ‘Wood Wide Web!’ This vast networks allows them to share nutrients and warn each other about droughts, pests and diseases. BEAUTIFUL REALMS

This place is my little garden on the side of the web, where I may talk about my ocs, stories, and my life and also see how much I can do with coding. Is mostly for archiving purposes but I would like to be part of communities this way; you may find me in many ways, but you must know that most of what you will see here is just for fun overall(and my way of expressing myself and connecting with others), wish you well!

This site also may contain 18+/mature content regarding some stuff talked about in stories and characters! I like dark themes in my stories, so. And maybe also the media log/art gallery(if I ever make a more 18+ version of it) But everything will be appropiately tagged!

My characters belong to a story called The Callous Miracle, which tells a story of a world similar to our own, an earth built from the body of a goddess called Tellus; but the most obvious difference is the presence of the supernatural and the gods in the modern day. The world is corrupted and full of cracks, from which lesser gods spawn, messing around with the lives of the mortals on it while they try to live in ignorence, going around the earth trying to keep a semblance of normality.

WHY OWN A SITE?

(or a short manifesto of sorts)

We shouldn't ask for permission to create. We shouldn't ask for licenses to speak. I won't ask for permission to exist, and neither should you. Owning a website of your own these days is an act of rebelion in itself!

Art should not be only a product, it is also political. It is a weapon. It is a scream. Creation is a gift and I won't allow anything take that power from me, this is why I always wanted to have a website of my own!

Self expression is important, specially in a world where the stablishment tries so much demonize and persecute people like us (the weirdos as you would say), and is a form of fighting back. This is my form of fighting back, and connecting with the community I am often deprived from.

WE ARE WHO WE ARE, AND APPROVAL IS NOT NEEDED!
